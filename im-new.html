Visiting our church for the first time, Here's everything you need to walk through our red doors with confidence.

A warm welcome!

When you first step into All Saints and Ascension you'll find a warm, welcoming community ready to share in the faith journey with you. Our greeters will meet you shortly after you walk through those red doors.

Just identify yourself as a visitor. The greeters will help you sign the guest registry, answer any questions, and get you seated. You'll be given a worship bulletin that guides you through the whole service.

Our worship follows the Book of Common Prayer and is "liturgical," which simply means it has a stable, familiar format, with times of standing, kneeling, sitting, and singing. If that feels new, don't worry: the bulletin walks you through every step.

Your questions, answered

Good things to know before your first visit.

Do I need to be a member to attend?

No. Everyone is welcome to worship and take part in our activities. We simply hope you'll consider whether a deeper life of faith within our community might be for you.

May I receive Communion?

Yes, all baptized Christians of any denomination are welcome to receive Holy Communion. When the usher releases your section, walk toward the altar. To receive the bread, extend your palms upward; when offered the chalice, gently help guide it.

How will I know what to do during the service?

You'll be handed a worship bulletin when you arrive. It guides you through the entire order of service, step by step.

What music will I hear?

Our Sunday 9 AM service includes music led by our choir. We sing from The Hymnal 1982, the "Lift Every Voice and Sing II" (LEVAS) African American hymnal, and "Lift Up Your Hearts." The Tuesday noonday service has no music.

Will my children be welcome?

Absolutely. Children are a joyful part of our worship, and we have a Children's Choir and free piano lessons for young ones in the community.
